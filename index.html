II. MISSION BRIEF

The purpose of this training program is to develop advanced cognitive operations capabilities in cadets, focusing on argument analysis, critique, and construction protocols. The program includes a comprehensive survey of essential logical frameworks and analytical tools required for strategic reasoning and decision-making processes.

These cognitive frameworks will be applied to examine select philosophical and scholarly transmissions from across the galaxy. Cadets will be required to compose several strategic argument analyses as part of their training evaluation.

Prerequisites: Reading & Writing Skills 2 (Basic Communications Protocol)
Note: If you disenroll from one of your co-requisite classes, you may be dropped from the other course.

III. TRAINING MATERIALS

IV. LEARNING OBJECTIVES

Upon completion of this training program, cadets will be able to:

  1. Comprehend components of logical arguments and strategic reasoning patterns.
  2. Acquire a general understanding of essential logical concepts needed for argument analysis, such as validity, soundness, deduction, and induction.
  3. Critically assess arguments with an aim toward identifying effective and reasonable strategic approaches.
  4. Learn to identify common logical fallacies and cognitive traps.
  5. Apply knowledge of argumentation principles to philosophical and scholarly transmissions.
  6. Learn to compose effective argument analyses and strategic communications.
Essential Skills: Critical Thinking, Personal & Social Responsibility, Information & Digital Literacy.

V. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

A. Training Components

This is a lecture-based training program with interactive discussion protocols. Cadets are expected to participate in strategic discussions within the context of assigned readings and respond to queries relevant to the training materials.

  • Online Assessment Protocols
  • Class Activities and Training Exercises
  • Argument Analysis Report
  • Strategic Argument Composition
  • Final Assessment (focused on Deductive Reasoning)

B. Attendance Protocol

All cadets enrolled for credit, credit/no credit, or audit status are expected to attend all training sessions. Attendance will be monitored. You are allowed up to three absences before your performance evaluation will be affected.

A cadet with excessive absences—15% of total class hours—may be removed from the program. Cadets should not assume automatic removal. Absences do not relieve cadets of responsibility for missed assignments and examinations.
